HTML Entities-
HTML entities are special codes or symbols used to represent characters that have special meanings or are not easily typed directly on a keyboard. They are often used to display reserved characters or characters that may have conflicting meanings in HTML or to display characters in non-Latin scripts. 

Here are five common HTML entities:

1. &lt; - Less Than Sign (<):
This entity represents the less than sign <. 

2. &gt; - Greater Than Sign (>):
This entity represents the greater than sign >. 

3. &amp; - Ampersand (&):
This entity represents the ampersand &.

4. &quot; - Double Quotation Mark ("):
This entity represents the double quotation mark ".

5. &apos; - Single Quotation Mark or Apostrophe ('):
This entity represents the single quotation mark or apostrophe '.
